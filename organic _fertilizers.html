Organic Fertilizers

Jeevamrutham

    Ingridents
  • 200 litres barallel
  • 10kg Fresh cow dung
  • 10 litres fresh cow urine/5lit old cow urine
  • 2kg brown sugar/jaggery
  • 2kg any flour
  • Healthy/virgin soil(100-150g)

    • Jeevamrutham Preparation Process

    Uses of Jeevamrutham
    Soil Improvement:

    Enhances Soil Fertility:
    Jeevamrutham is a rich source of nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorus, calcium, and micronutrients.
    Increases Microbial Activity:
    It boosts the activity of beneficial soil microorganisms, which are crucial for nutrient availability and overall soil health.
    Improves Soil Structure:
    It helps in the decomposition of organic matter, making nutrients more accessible to plants and improving soil structure.
    Balances Soil pH:
    Jeevamrutham can help adjust the soil's pH to a more favorable level for plant growth.
    Retains Soil Moisture:
    It can improve the soil's ability to retain moisture, reducing the need for frequent watering.

    Plant Growth and Yield:

    Promotes Plant Growth:
    By providing essential nutrients and improving soil conditions, Jeevamrutham encourages healthy plant growth and development.
    Enhances Flowering and Fruiting:
    It can contribute to better flowering and fruiting, leading to increased yields.
    Strengthens Plant Immunity:
    The beneficial microbes in Jeevamrutham can help improve plant immunity, making them more resistant to diseases and pests.
    Reduces Reliance on Chemicals:
    Jeevamrutham is a sustainable and eco-friendly alternative to chemical fertilizers, promoting a healthier environment.
    Cost-Effective:
    It can be prepared at a low cost using readily available materials, making it a budget-friendly option for farmers and gardeners.

    Other Uses:

    Vermicomposting:
    Jeevamrutham is a key component in vermicomposting, enhancing the quality of the resulting organic fertilizer.
    Crop Application:
    It can be applied to the soil or diluted with water for foliar spray, depending on the crop and stage of growth.
    Traditional Practice:
    Jeevamrutham is a traditional Indian farming practice, embraced for its effectiveness in promoting sustainable and organic agriculture.

    Fish-tonic/Fish Amina Acid

      Ingridents
  • Plastic Drum with closing tap/Clay Pot
  • Fish Waste
  • Brown Sugar/Black Jaggery
  • Banana/waste Fruits(Optional)

    • fish-tonic/Fish Amina Acid Preparation Process

    1.Plant Growth and Development:
    Nutrient Supply:
    FAA is a rich source of nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium, which are vital for plant growth and development.
    Improved Nutrient Uptake:
    It helps plants absorb water and nutrients more efficiently, leading to better overall nutrient utilization.
    Enhanced Photosynthesis:
    FAA promotes photosynthesis, the process by which plants convert light energy into chemical energy, supporting plant growth.
    Faster Growth Rates:
    Plants absorb amino acids quickly, leading to faster growth and healthier foliage, especially for seedlings.
    Improved Root Development:
    It enhances root growth, allowing plants to access more nutrients and water from the soil.
    Increased Yields:
    Studies have shown that FAA can lead to increased crop yields.
    2. Soil Health:
    Soil Fertility:
    FAA helps to improve soil fertility by enriching it with essential nutrients.
    Soil Structure:
    It can improve soil structure, making it more loose and porous, which is beneficial for root growth and water retention.
    Microbial Activity:
    FAA stimulates the activity of beneficial soil microorganisms, which play a crucial role in nutrient cycling and soil health.
    3. Pest and Disease Resistance:
    Insect Repellent:
    Some studies suggest that FAA can help to repel certain insects, like worm moths and mites.
    Disease Resistance:
    By improving overall plant health, FAA can indirectly enhance a plant's resistance to diseases.
    4. Other Uses:
    Cost-Effective:
    FAA can be made from readily available materials like fish waste and sugar, making it a cost-effective fertilizer option for farmers.
    Sustainable:
    It's considered a sustainable approach to plant nutrition as it utilizes waste products.
    Home Gardening:
    FAA can be used in home gardens to improve the health and productivity of plants.
